Bob Ross Painting Guide

  1. Gather Supplies
    • Oil paints: Bob Ross typically used oil paints.
    • Brushes: Large brushes for backgrounds, fan brushes for trees, and smaller brushes for details.
    • Palette knife: For creating mountains and other textured elements.
    • Canvas: Usually a pre-stretched canvas.
  2. Prepare Your Canvas: Apply a thin coat of liquid white or liquid clear to your canvas. This helps the paint blend smoothly.
  3. Start with the Background: Use large brushes to paint the sky and water. Blend colors directly on the canvas using crisscross strokes.
  4. Add Mountains: Use a palette knife to apply dark paint for the base of the mountains. Scrape off excess paint to create texture, then add highlights with lighter colors.
  5. Create Trees and Foliage: Use fan brushes to paint trees. Start with dark colors for the base and add lighter colors for highlights. Use tapping motions to create leaves and bushes.
  6. Add Details: Use smaller brushes to add details like reflections in water, grass, and other elements.
  7. Final Touches: Add any final details and highlights to make your painting pop. Bob Ross often added "happy little trees" and other elements to bring his paintings to life.
